Quick Tips

Common mistakes and things to double-check before you submit.

🗣️

Writing Style

Write in third person, past tense throughout. Avoid "I", "we", or "you". Exception: the Conclusion and Improvements may use future tense for suggestions.

📏

Word Count

Maximum 2000 words. Numbers, data tables, and figures are excluded from the count. Mrs Biddle will only comment on up to 2000 words in your draft.

📓

Visual Journal

This is compulsory — include planning notes, diagrams, photos of your experiment, data collection sheets, and reflections at each checkpoint.

🔢

Statistics Checklist

Make sure you have: normality check, mean, median, SD, IQR, SE, confidence intervals, a statistical test (t-test), p-value, and a decision on the null hypothesis.

📈

Your Graph

Column graph with means (not raw data) and confidence intervals as error bars. Label figures below and tables above. Every figure needs a description in the text.

⚖️

The Key Word: "Justified"

To reach 4–5 marks in most criteria, your response needs to be "justified" — not just described. Always explain why each decision was made.

🔗

Link Everything Back

Your conclusion must directly answer your research question. Your improvements must come from your data, not generic ideas. Your rationale must link to your modification.

📝

Referencing

APA 7th edition. Every in-text citation = a reference list entry, and vice versa. Must include the original study. References start on a new page.

✍️

What's a "Modification"?

Your experiment must be genuinely different from the original study — not just the same study repeated. Use: Refine (improve methodology), Extend (add new variable/population), or Redirect (change focus).

🧠

About the Original Study

Your experiment modifies Grant et al. (1998) — a study on context-dependent memory for meaningful material (studying and testing in silent vs noisy environments). Make sure you read the full paper and understand its aim, methodology, results, and conclusions before selecting your modification.
