1. Title

5–10 words · Same as your research question in statement form
▼
Rules: Not bolded, not centred, no full stop, less than 15 words, title-case capitalised. Names your IV and DV.

Template: "The Effect of [IV] on [DV] in [Target Population]"

📖

2. Rationale

200–250 words · 3+ in-text citations · Inverted triangle structure
▼
Think of it as an inverted triangle — start broad (background theory), narrow down to the original study, then to your specific modification.
  • Para 1 — "Hook me" (Background): Define key psychological terms, your IV and DV, and identify a societal problem that makes this research relevant. Include 3+ in-text citations.
  • Para 2 — "Teach me" (Theory): Explain the background theory (e.g. context-dependent memory, encoding specificity, environmental context effects). Link concepts to memory research.
  • Para 3 — "Remind me" (Original Study): Briefly describe the original study — sample, operationalised variables, results, and identify a gap or limitation. Include this as your 4th citation. Name all aspects you plan to modify.
  • Para 4 — "Convince me" (Modified Study): Describe your modifications (use "Modified Study"), justify each with an external source, and tie off to your research question.

Modifications marked on:

✓ Justified = 2 marks ≈ Feasible = 1 mark ✗ Inappropriate = 0 marks
❓

3. Research Question & Hypotheses

50–100 words · RQ + Null + Alternative
▼
  • Research Question: Must be specific (IV, DV, target population), relevant, investigable, and phrased as a closed yes/no question. Start with: "To what extent…" / "How does…" / "Does…" / "What effect does…"
  • Null Hypothesis: States there is no significant difference/effect between conditions.
  • Alternative Hypothesis: Must be specific (operationalised IV and DV), directional (predicts which group will score higher/lower), testable and falsifiable.
✓ Specific & relevant = top mark ≈ Relevant = mid mark ✗ Inappropriate = low mark
🔬

4. Methodology

Design 100–150 w · Participants 50–100 w · Ethics 50 w
▼

Three sub-sections. Write in past tense. Step-by-step procedure goes in an appendix — not the body.

① Experimental Design & Variables (100–150 words)

  • State the research design (repeated measures or independent groups) and justify why
  • Operationalise the IV (what levels/conditions you used)
  • Describe how the DV was measured (units, tool, scale)
  • List control variables (EVs you controlled)
  • Describe modifications in detail — use terms Refinement, Extension, or Redirection

② Participants (50–100 words)

  • Describe target population and your sample (age, year level, location)
  • State recruitment method and sampling technique (convenience, random, stratified)
  • Justify sampling method
  • Compare to the original study's participants — explain any changes
  • State how participants were allocated to groups (if independent groups design)

③ Safety & Ethics (~50 words)

Don't just list ethics — connect them to your study. The ISMG rewards considered management. For each ethical issue, explain specifically how it was managed in your experiment and why it mattered given your modification.

❌ Listing (low marks)

"Informed consent was obtained. Participants had the right to withdraw. Confidentiality was maintained."

✅ Considered management (top marks)

"Because participants were exposed to background noise during the study phase, informed consent was obtained prior to participation so they were aware of the conditions. Participants were reminded of their right to withdraw at any time without consequence. Confidentiality was maintained by assigning each participant a code number rather than recording names."

Ask yourself for each ethical consideration:

  • Why does this ethical principle apply to my specific study?
  • What did I actually do to manage it — not just that I did it, but how?
  • Does my modification create any new ethical considerations the original study didn't have?

Write in past tense. Include consent forms in appendix as supporting evidence. Refer to the ISMG — top marks require considered management, not just identification.

✓ Considered management = top mark ≈ Management = mid mark ✗ Inadequate = low mark
📊

5. Results

~300 words · Tables & figures excluded from word count
▼

Four sub-sections — report only, do not interpret (save that for Discussion).

5.1 Raw Data

One sentence introducing the table. Table 1 with all raw data — layout must match your experimental design. Heading above the table.

5.2 Descriptive Statistics (100–150 words)

  • Check for normality (histogram or Kolmogorov-Smirnov) → choose mean or median accordingly
  • Calculate: M or Mdn, SD or IQR, SEM, confidence intervals
  • Table 2 — include mean, SD, SEM, sample size for each condition
  • Figure 1 — column/bar graph with means + confidence interval error bars, labelled axes, B&W
  • Write 100–150 words: which measure chosen and why, compare group means, compare SDs, comment on error bar overlap and what it means
Sentence template: "On average, participants had [higher/lower] [DV] in the [condition 1] (M = __) than the [condition 2] (M = __)."

5.3 Inferential Statistics (100–150 words)

  • State the statistical test used (e.g. independent samples t-test) and justify why (research design, data type, normality, outliers)
  • Check assumptions: level of measurement, independence, normality, equal variance
  • Report t-value and p-value; add to Table 2
  • State whether the result is statistically significant (p < 0.05 or p ≥ 0.05)
  • Accept or reject the null hypothesis
Sentence template: "There is/is not a statistically significant difference (p = __) in [DV] between [condition 1] and [condition 2] because the p-value is lower/higher than 0.05. This means that [IV] does/does not affect [DV] for [target population]."

Data processing marked on:

✓ Correct & relevant = top mark ≈ Basic = mid mark ✗ Incorrect/irrelevant = low mark
🔍

6. Discussion

500–600 words · Trends · Limitations · Reliability & Validity
▼

Three parts — analysis of results, then evaluation through reliability & validity.

Part 1 — Analyse Results

  • First sentence = biggest finding most useful for answering the RQ
  • Describe trends/patterns using words: increased, decreased, linear, gradually, sharply
  • Reference specific data values from your tables/figures
  • Link results to psychological theory (context-dependent memory, encoding specificity) and to the original study (Grant et al., 1998)
Uncertainty template: "The [condition] scores had a greater dispersion (SD = __) than the [condition] (SD = __) which increases the uncertainty of the results for the [condition] scores."

Part 2 — Limitations (with structure: Identify → Describe → Evaluate → Improve)

Common limitations for this type of experiment:

Sample bias — convenience sampling → unrepresentative of target population → lowers external (population) validity → improve with random/stratified sampling
Order effects (repeated measures only) — practice/fatigue effects → confounding variable → lowers internal validity → improve with counterbalancing
Demand characteristics — participants guess the aim → alter behaviour → lowers internal validity → improve with single-blind procedure
Lack of mundane realism — artificial task → lowers external validity → improve with more realistic stimulus

Part 3 — Reliability & Validity (include BOTH types of each)

  • External reliability: Would repeating the experiment produce similar results? (Test-retest, inter-rater)
  • Internal reliability: Is your instrument/measure internally consistent?
  • Internal validity: Were effects due to your IV, or could confounding variables explain them?
  • External validity: Can your findings generalise beyond this specific sample/setting? (population validity, ecological validity)
  • Include both strengths and weaknesses — don't only list problems

Reliability & validity marked on:

✓ Justified discussion = top mark ≈ Reasonable description = mid mark ✗ Cursory/simplistic = low mark
✅

7. Conclusion

50–100 words · Directly answers the RQ · Include p-value
▼
Must include: restate the RQ, state the statistical result (include p-value), state whether null hypothesis was accepted or rejected, and acknowledge key limitations.
  • Restate the aim/RQ
  • Summarise findings — use the word "significant" where appropriate
  • Compare to the original study and link to theory
  • Explicitly answer the research question
  • Do NOT introduce new information here
✓ Justified & linked to RQ = top mark ≈ Reasonable & relevant = mid mark ✗ Inappropriate/irrelevant = low mark
🔧

8. Improvements & Extensions

300–400 words · 2–4 improvements + at least 1 extension
▼
Golden rule: Every improvement must solve a problem already identified in your Discussion. Do NOT suggest an improvement without first identifying the limitation it fixes.
  • For each improvement: (1) Name the limitation, (2) State the improvement, (3) Explain the effect of the change, (4) State how it improves reliability or validity
  • Include at least 2 improvements from your Discussion limitations
  • Include at least 1 extension — a new direction for future research beyond fixing current problems

Example structure:

"The use of convenience sampling limited the external validity of this study because… An improvement would be to use random sampling, which would produce a more representative sample of the target population and increase external (population) validity."

✓ Logically derived from analysis = top mark ≈ Related to analysis = mid mark ✗ Ineffective/irrelevant = low mark
📚

9–10. Word Count & References

Word count required at end · References on new page · APA 7th
▼
  • State your word count at the end of Section 9
  • References begin on a new page as Section 10
  • Heading: References — bold, centred
  • Double-spaced, hanging indent, alphabetical order
  • Every in-text citation = one reference list entry (and vice versa)
  • The original study (Grant et al., 1998) must be included in your reference list

See the 🔖 APA Guide tab for full reference formats (journal, book, webpage, dictionary, etc.)
