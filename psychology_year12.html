Unit Summaries

An overview of what you'll study in Units 3 and 4 this year.

3
Individual Thinking
Brain Function · Sensation · Memory · Learning

Explore how psychology describes and explains the role of the human nervous system in thinking, and the cognitive processes involved in perception, memory and learning.

  • T1Brain Function — nervous system, neurotransmitters, Parkinson's & Alzheimer's10 hrs
  • T2Sensation & Perception — seeing, hearing, perceptual set, Gestalt, cultural influences10 hrs
  • T3Memory — multi-store, working memory, LOP models; encoding, retrieval, forgetting15 hrs
  • T4Learning — classical conditioning, operant conditioning, social learning theory10 hrs
4
The Influence of Others
Social Psych · Interpersonal · Attitudes · Cross-Cultural

Investigate how others influence our development, behaviour and thinking. Examine social processes in relationships, stereotypes, attitude formation and change, and cross-cultural perspectives.

  • T1Social Psychology — socialisation, gender, conformity, obedience (Asch, Milgram, Zimbardo)10 hrs
  • T2Interpersonal Processes — prosocial behaviour, bystander effect, aggression, attraction, relationships15 hrs
  • T3Attitudes — tri-component model, cognitive dissonance, social identity, prejudice & discrimination10 hrs
  • T4Cross-Cultural Psychology — culture, acculturation, multiculturalism, reducing prejudice10 hrs

Syllabus Objectives

Six objectives apply across both units. Every assessment task is aligned to a subset of these objectives.

1
Describe
Use scientific representations and language in appropriate genres to give a detailed account of phenomena, concepts, theories, models and systems.
2
Apply Understanding
Use scientific concepts, theories and models within their limitations. Use algebraic, visual and graphical representations to determine unknown quantities or explain phenomena.
3
Analyse Data
Consider information from primary and secondary sources to identify trends, patterns, relationships, limitations and uncertainty in qualitative and quantitative data.
4
Interpret Evidence
Use scientific understanding to draw conclusions, develop scientific arguments, deduce, extrapolate, infer, justify and make predictions from analysis of data.
5
Evaluate
Critically reflect on available evidence and judge its application to research questions. Evaluate validity, reliability and suggest improvements. (Internal assessments only.)
6
Investigate
Develop rationales and research questions. Modify methodologies to collect primary data, manage risks and ethical issues, and acknowledge sources. (Internal assessments only.)
